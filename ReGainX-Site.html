Our Mission

ReGainX was founded to close the mobility gap worldwide. We blend adaptive robotics with everyday practicality to support people with motor impairments, caregivers who assist them, and clinicians seeking better tools.

We focus on three promises: inclusive design, affordability, and measurable quality-of-life impact. Our founding story began with family caregivers looking for an alternative to costly clinical devices—today we collaborate with global non-profits, rehab centers, and early adopters to deliver solutions that matter.

Who Benefits?

  • Individuals with motor impairments or progressive conditions
  • Clinicians, PT/OT professionals, and rehab programs
  • Non-profits supporting mobility equity worldwide

Product & How It Works

Key features

  • Multi-joint adaptive actuators with sensor fusion for smoother motion.
  • Smart controller that learns gait patterns to provide tailored assistance.
  • Lightweight modular frame for dressing independence and easy maintenance.
  • Safety redundancies: angle limits, torque limits, energy efficiency.

How it works

ReGainX uses embedded sensors along elbows, hips, knees, or lower back to capture motion intent in real time. Algorithms translate that data into responsive torque support, providing extra strength precisely when it's needed. Rechargeable batteries last through daily routines, and the system integrates with tele-rehab dashboards for clinicians.

Benefits

Improved endurance, reduced caregiver strain, and faster rehab progress—all within an approachable price point.

Software & Control Intelligence

The ReGainX software stack fuses simulator insights with on-body sensors, enabling fast tuning, digital twins, and clinician dashboards. Our adaptive control engine is validated in simulation before deploying to real hardware, shortening iteration cycles.

  • Edge AI models detect intent and micro-adjust torque outputs every 200ms.
  • Cloud console lets therapists configure modes, monitor usage, and push updates securely.
  • Simulator fidelity ensures what we test virtually performs identically on the exoskeleton.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ReGainX safe for daily use?

Yes. The exoskeleton includes non-invasive sensors, torque limits, and auto-stop features.

Who can use it?

Designed for adults with mobility impairments or strength loss, primarily designed for Parkinson's patients; clinicians determine suitability during onboarding.

How much will it cost?

Final pricing will be announced ahead of launch, targeting a fraction of current clinical exoskeletons with financing options. Currenlty Elbow exoskeleton is nearing $130, while lower-limb exoskeletons are in development

What about sizing?

Modular frames and straps adjust for a wide range of body types, and fittings can be done at home or in clinics. The exoskeleton is designed to be used by anyone, regardless of age, gender, or body type.

Do I need training?

No, however a short onboarding session is recommended; remote coaching and video guides will be included.

Maintenance requirements?

Tool-free module swaps, washable liners, and quarterly software updates pushed over Wi-Fi.

When will it ship?

Pilot shipments begin next year with rolling availability by region; join the waitlist for updates.

Can clinicians monitor progress?

Yes. Secure dashboards track gait metrics, battery health, and adherence for care teams.

Does it work outdoors?

Weather-resistant materials and sealed components support outdoor walking and commuting.
